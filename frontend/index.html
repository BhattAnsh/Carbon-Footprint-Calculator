What is Carbon footprint?

Carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gas emissions, primarily carbon dioxide, that are generated by an individual, organization, or product. These emissions are caused by activities such as transportation, energy use, and manufacturing. Carbon footprint is a critical measure of the impact of human activities on the environment, and reducing it is essential for combating climate change. By making changes in our daily habits and adopting sustainable practices, we can all work towards reducing our carbon footprint and creating a more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
